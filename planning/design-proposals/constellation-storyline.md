# Constellation Storyline

## Concept name
Constellation Storyline

## Visual mood
A calm, artful long-scroll experience that feels handcrafted without becoming whimsical. The tone is reflective, polished, and slightly cinematic. It takes inspiration from the spirit of an illustrated personal web page with memorable link moments, but adapts that idea into a cleaner, more contemporary developer portfolio.

## Layout structure
- Long single-page layout with gentle section transitions
- Intro hero with a short personal statement and primary links
- Project moments presented as illustrated or diagram-like waypoints along the scroll
- Light parallax or subtle motion to create rhythm, not spectacle
- Clear footer with resume, LinkedIn, GitHub, and contact links

## Key sections
- Intro / personal headline
- Selected projects as narrative highlights
- "How I like to build" or working principles
- Short about section with photo or minimal portrait treatment
- Resume and professional links
- Contact / next step CTA

## Color and typography direction
- Palette: deep midnight blue, soft fog gray, warm off-white, small accents of muted gold or lilac
- Typography: refined sans-serif for body, paired with a tasteful display serif or humanist heading font
- Visual accents: thin line illustrations, subtle star-map or system-diagram motifs

## Strengths and tradeoffs
### Strengths
- Most memorable and original direction
- Communicates personality and thoughtfulness
- Gives projects an emotional arc rather than just listing them

### Tradeoffs
- Requires more design craft to avoid feeling overly decorative
- Longer scroll may hide some information from fast skimmers
- Custom illustrations or visual motifs add implementation time

## Best fit
Best if Skyler wants a portfolio that feels personal, mature, and creative while still remaining professional for software work.
