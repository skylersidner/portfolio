# Studio Casebook

## Concept name
Studio Casebook

## Visual mood
Editorial and thoughtful, like a small digital design-and-engineering journal. It presents Skyler as someone who not only ships code, but also frames problems clearly and makes solid product decisions.

## Layout structure
- Structured homepage with a strong introductory block
- 2 to 4 featured project case studies with richer summaries
- Secondary archive for smaller experiments and ideas
- Repeating case-study template with challenge, approach, outcome, and links
- Optional writing or notes section to show thinking

## Key sections
- Introduction
- Featured case studies
- Experiments / side ideas
- Notes or writing
- About / working style
- Resume and external links

## Color and typography direction
- Palette: ivory or paper white, ink black, muted navy, soft clay or olive accent
- Typography: editorial serif for headings with a highly readable sans-serif for body copy
- Design cues: asymmetrical layouts, pull quotes, understated dividers, image captions

## Strengths and tradeoffs
### Strengths
- Excellent for showing process and product thinking
- Makes even a smaller project list feel substantial
- Distinguishes Skyler as a reflective builder, not just a coder

### Tradeoffs
- Needs solid writing to fully shine
- Slightly more content-heavy than a simple portfolio landing page

## Best fit
Best if Skyler wants employers or collaborators to understand how he thinks, not just what he built.
